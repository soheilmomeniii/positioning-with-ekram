# Glossary

Use this reference when exact steering language matters.

## Second Existence

The story the market tells about the product, separate from the product reality. The market acts on this story.

## Legible, Desirable, Inevitable

The three jobs of marketing:

- legible: understood
- desirable: wanted
- inevitable: believed as a direction

## Four Questions

What is it? Why does it matter? Why this, not the substitute? Why now?

## Weak Equals No

If differentiation, relevance, or authenticity is weak, the position is not ready.

## Sacrifice Must Hurt

A real position gives up audience, revenue, features, status, claims, or optionality. Painless sacrifice is not sacrifice.

## Jordan Way

Compete inside an existing category and win on a visible axis.

## Thiel Way

Avoid direct competition by creating or owning a new category or frame.

## Middle Is Mush

The unclear straddle where a company claims to be both better inside an existing category and a totally new category.

## Fork Test

The question "can this advantage be copied?" If yes, it is not the moat.

## Brand Is Earned

Brand is the emotional response created by repeated behavior, not a feeling a company can simply declare.

## One Emotion

The primary emotional charge the brand should earn.

## Belonging As Moat

Community identity becomes defensibility when users defend and repeat the meaning themselves.

## Narrative Singularity

Holding one narrative long enough for the market to remember and repeat it.

## Future Delta

What becomes meaningfully different if this wins.

## Enemy Frame

The old belief, behavior, institution, category, architecture, or status quo that the narrative makes obsolete.

## Early, Not Evil

A way to frame incumbents as historically understandable but no longer right for the new world.

## Founder As Symbol

The founder as a human container for the company's belief, taste, risk, and fight.

## Rizz

The quality that makes the market look twice. Attention earned through taste, timing, tension, humor, compression, or public risk.

## Bundle And Breakaway

Attach to a live narrative to borrow attention, then separate with a sharper owned frame.

## Meme The Gigabrain

Compress a complex idea into a phrase, diagram, line, or ritual the market can repeat.

## Phased Inevitability

A launch sequence that makes momentum feel progressively obvious rather than suddenly hyped.

## Dunk Risk

The chance that a public claim invites easy mockery, contradiction, or screenshot-driven collapse.

## Coordination Signal

The repeatable line or proof that lets believers coordinate around the same story.

## The Name Is The Thesis

A strong name encodes the whole pitch before a feature is explained.

## The List Is The Positioning

The coalition you assemble is itself the argument; the backers you gather are the claim.

## No-FUD (Indifference As Failure)

The absence of any attack or argument is the signature of irrelevance. If no one bothers to push back, there is no position yet.

## Turn Your Case Into A Category Question

Reframe your specific moment as the question the whole category has to answer.

## Alarm Vs Hype

Alarm makes a real, near-term downside visible; hype inflates a reward that may never arrive. Only the first is honest urgency.

## Prove Before You Say

At launch, stage the proof, the list, the coalition, and the phase so the market reads them before the words.

## Months Are Noise, Years Are Truth

Judge a narrative over years, not cycles; drift resets the clock.

## Who Shows Up

The final judge of a position: the people it attracts. Judge positioning by who arrives, joins, and repeats it, not by applause or impressions.

## Strategic Territories

Positioning directions written while proof is still missing: 2 or 3 honest candidate positions, each with its implied sacrifice and the proof needed to earn it. Strategy without a single fake claim. Territories are not copy and carry no final headlines.

## Provisional Copy

Copy written before the proof gate is passed, only at the user's explicit request. Always labeled as unproven, claims shrunk to what is honest today, proof spine attached. A bridge, never a destination.
