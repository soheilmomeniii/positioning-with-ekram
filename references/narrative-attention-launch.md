## Narrative Singularity

A company should hold one narrative long enough for the market to repeat it.

Narrative is not a slogan. It is a story about how the world changes and why this thing belongs to that change.

Narrative components:

- old world
- broken assumption
- new force
- new world
- enemy
- future delta
- repeatable line

## Future Delta

The future delta is what becomes meaningfully different if this wins.

Weak:

- "better user experience"
- "more accessible"
- "AI-powered workflow"

Stronger:

- "uncertainty becomes tradable before it becomes binary"
- "small teams can look institution-grade without hiring a department"
- "developers stop buying the whole stack just to use one layer"

## Enemy Frame

Every strong narrative has tension. The enemy may be:

- old category
- old behavior
- hidden tax
- inefficient institution
- fake consensus
- incumbent frame
- outdated technical architecture
- lazy market assumption

Avoid cartoon villains. Strong enemies are often historical: they made sense before, but now they are early, not evil, or obsolete.

## Attention

Attention is either created or borrowed.

Create attention when the company has original taste, founder charisma, cultural sharpness, or a native media instinct.

Borrow attention when the company can attach itself to a larger conversation, then break away with its own position.

Do not buy attention as a substitute for meaning.

## Rizz

Rizz is the ability to make the market look twice.

It can come from:

- tension
- elegance
- humor
- timing
- taste
- status reversal
- an unusually compressed line
- a public bet
- a new word that makes old reality legible

Rizz without position is empty spectacle.

## Bundle And Breakaway

Bundle with a live narrative to borrow attention. Break away before becoming a footnote.

Pattern:

1. Name the live conversation.
2. Attach to it with a clear relation.
3. Introduce the sharper frame.
4. Make the frame repeatable.
5. Stop depending on the borrowed narrative.

## Meme The Gigabrain

If the idea is complex, create a simple handle the market can repeat.

Do not dumb it down into nonsense. Compress the idea until it travels.

Useful forms:

- one line
- one enemy
- one diagram
- one phrase
- one comparison
- one ritual

## Distribution

Distribution is sustained delivery of the position.

Choose the pipes:

- founder account
- company account
- community
- partners
- investors
- customers
- press
- newsletters
- podcasts
- demos
- conferences
- public docs

The same position should show up differently across each pipe, not become a new strategy in every channel.

## Launches

A launch is concentrated delivery.

Use phased inevitability:

1. Problem signal: make the pain visible.
2. Proof signal: show credible substance.
3. Coalition signal: show who believes.
4. Product signal: reveal the thing.
5. Momentum signal: show adoption, not hype.

Manage dunk risk:

- Do not overclaim before proof.
- Do not choose a claim that one screenshot can destroy.
- Do not make the launch depend on fake scarcity.
- Do not invite a comparison you cannot win.

Coordination signal:

- Give believers the line to repeat.
- Make the repeatable line short enough to travel.
- Make it specific enough to avoid generic hype.

## Crisis

Crisis reveals whether the brand was real.

Respond by:

1. Naming what happened plainly.
2. Taking the right amount of responsibility.
3. Reconnecting to the position.
4. Showing what changes.
5. Avoiding defensive cleverness.

Do not use narrative to hide reality. In crisis, reality wins.
